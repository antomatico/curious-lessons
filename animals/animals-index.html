Where Does a Sheep Come From?

A true story about animal families, a clever man named Darwin, and how a wild mountain animal became the woolly sheep on a Kiwi hillside.

🐑 Made for every curious Kiwi school kid · about 4 short lessons

Let's begin↓

It starts with one good question

A boy looked at a fluffy sheep and asked his dad something brilliant: "Where does a sheep come from?" It turns out that little question opens up one of the biggest, most amazing stories in all of science.

"Where does a sheep come from?" 🤔
By the end of these lessons, you'll be able to answer it yourself — all the way back to a wild animal from 11,000 years ago!

Your four lessons

Do them in order — each one helps the next make sense.

Where Does a Sheep Really Come From?

The big one! A wild, hairy mountain animal called the mouflon slowly became the soft, woolly sheep — because people kept choosing the fluffiest ones for thousands of years.

Aotearoa's Animals

Native, wild, tame and farmed — sort out New Zealand's animals, and discover how the sheep finally made it all the way to our green hills.

This week in history: Captain Cook let the very first sheep loose in New Zealand on 20 May 1773 — that's almost exactly 253 years ago this week! (Sadly those first two sheep ate the wrong plants and didn't last long. You'll hear the full story in Lesson 4.)
