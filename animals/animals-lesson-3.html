Lesson 3

Where Does a Sheep Really Come From?

The answer to the boy's question! A wild, hairy mountain animal slowly became the soft, woolly sheep — because of a clever trick people discovered long ago.

Follow the sheep↓
The Big Idea

A wild animal + thousands of years of people choosing = the sheep

Today's fluffy sheep came from a tough, hairy wild mountain animal called the mouflon. It had no fluffy wool at all! Over thousands of years, people kept the woolliest, gentlest sheep and bred them — slowly turning a wild animal into the woolly sheep we know. 🐑

Remember Darwin in Lesson 2? In nature, nature does the choosing. But with sheep, people did the choosing instead. Same idea — different chooser!

Meet the great-great-grandparent

The wild mouflon 🐏

Long, long ago there was no such thing as a fluffy sheep. Instead there was a wild animal called the mouflon (say it: MOO-flon). It lived on rocky mountains and it was nothing like a cuddly farm sheep:

🐏

It had big curved horns

Sharp, twisty horns for fighting and showing off — not soft and cuddly at all!

🟤

It had a coarse brown coat

A rough, hairy coat — NOT thick white wool. And it shed its coat each spring, just like a dog.

💨

It was fast and shy

Wild mouflon could run and leap across mountains to escape danger.

The whole story

How the mouflon became a sheep ⏳

About 11,000 years ago

People start keeping wild sheep 🏕️

In a place called the Fertile Crescent (today that's countries like Turkey, Iraq and Iran), people stopped only hunting wild mouflon and started keeping them for milk, meat and skins. Keeping wild animals and slowly taming them is called domestication.

Over many lifetimes

People pick the best ones 👨‍🌾

Farmers noticed some sheep were calmer, or had softer, thicker coats. They let those sheep have babies. The babies were a bit woollier too. Do that again and again for hundreds of years…

About 8,000 years ago (around 6000 BCE)

Wool appears! 🧶

Slowly the rough hair turned into thick, soft wool that kept growing instead of falling out. It took about 3,000 to 4,000 years of careful choosing to make a truly woolly sheep!

Today

The sheep we know 🐑

Now there are hundreds of kinds of sheep, all over the world — and they need people to shear (cut) their wool, because they can't shed it anymore.

When people do the choosing like this, it has a special name: artificial selection. ("Artificial" just means "made by people.") It's Darwin's idea — but with humans as the chooser.

The same trick, again

People did this to wolves too! 🐺➡️🐕

Here's something amazing: people used the exact same trick to turn wild wolves into pet dogs — a very, very long time ago. They kept the friendliest wolves, who had friendlier puppies, and slowly… you get the chihuahua, the labrador, and every dog you've ever met!

So a poodle and a woolly sheep have something in common: both are a wild animal + lots and lots of human choosing. 🐩🐑

📜
A bit of history: Wool changed the world! Once people had woolly sheep, they could make warm clothes, blankets and cloth to trade. Wool helped whole cities and countries grow rich — long before there were shops or money like ours.
🥝
Aotearoa connection: The woolly sheep that all this choosing created is exactly the animal that would later be carried by ship all the way to New Zealand. How did it get here, and why are there SO many? That's the story of Lesson 4! 🚢
